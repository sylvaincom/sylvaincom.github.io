In my first blog post on <a href="https://towardsdatascience.com/" target="_blank">Towards Data Science</a>, featured as an Editor's Pick and in the Getting Started Column by the editorial board, I try to convey the basics of what I learned about GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks) looking back on a 3-month research internship I did. How do GANs work? Why are they so interesting?

GANs are a deep learning method introduced in 2014. For example, they can create realistic-looking yet entirely computer generated photos of people's faces. GANs are also behind the controversial deepfake videos. Actually, they can imitate any data distribution (image, text, sound, etc).
